Welcome to The Gothic

Gothic Writing was first founded in the historical and sociological events of the late 18th and early 19th centuries. "Gothic" originates from the name of an ancient Germanic tribe (Goths) who are believed to contribute to the fall of the Roman Empire. Good luck.

Examples of Themes & Scenes in Gothic Literature

Typical gothic scenes include: A graveyard, dungeon, forest, abandoned castle, ghost towns.

Typical characters include: villans, fallen heros, a hero, religious figures and a victim.

Typical genre features include: fog, night, misty, not being able to see

Gothic author descriptions

Ann Radcliffe (1764 - 1823)
Ann Radcliffe was a English novelist known for pioneering the Gothic. Her most famous works include "The mysteries of Udolpho" (1794) and "The Italian" (1797). She combined themes of the supernatural with suspenseful narratives, making the narratives successful.
Mary Shelly (1797 - 1851)
Mary Shelly was an English novelist famously known for her seminal work (to strongly develop on the plot). She made "Frankenstein" (1818) (or the "The Modern Prometheus"). She is considered to be an early role model of science fiction.
Edgar Allan Poe(1809 - 1849)
Edgar Allan Poe was a pioneering American writer known for "The Murders in the Rue Morgue" (1841) and "The Tell-Tale Heart" (1843). He majorly influenced literature, in the themes of mystery and horror.
Bram Stoker (1847 - 1912)
Bram Stoker was an Irish novelist and theater manager, famously knowns for writing the Gothic "Dracula" (1897) as a horror novel, which is considered to be the best novel in vampire literature. He also managed the Lyceum Theatre in London from 1878 to 1904.
Oscar Wilde (1854 - 1900)
Oscar Wilde was an Irish author, poet and playright, best for his famous novel, "The picture of Dorian Gray" (1890). He used themes from the Gothic like magic picture, duality and physical mutability.
Horace Walpole (1717 - 1797)
Horace Walpole was an English politician, writer, historian and antiquarian. He is famous for building the famous Strawberry Hill House (built in Twickenham, Southwest London) and reviving the Gothic style. He is well known for his novel "The castle of Otranto" (1764)
